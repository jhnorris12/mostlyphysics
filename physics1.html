Kinematics & Motion

Physics I

How do you describe motion mathematically — position, velocity, and acceleration — without worrying about what caused it? Kinematics is the foundation every other mechanics topic is built on. The key skill is knowing which of the four kinematic equations to use based on what variables you know and what you're solving for.

Newton's Laws & Forces

Physics I

How do you apply Newton's Second Law to a system with multiple forces? The process is always the same: draw the free body diagram first, identify every force and its direction, write ΣF = ma in each direction, then solve. These videos walk through that process for inclined planes, Atwood machines, friction problems, and more.

Energy & Momentum

Physics I

The work-energy theorem and conservation of energy give you a powerful shortcut for problems where forces vary or you only care about initial and final states. Momentum conservation handles collisions. Knowing when to use energy methods vs. Newton's law methods is one of the most important judgment calls in Physics I.

Rotation & Oscillations

Physics I

Rotational mechanics is Newton's laws applied to spinning objects — torque is the rotational equivalent of force, moment of inertia replaces mass, and angular acceleration replaces linear acceleration. Simple harmonic motion describes oscillating systems like springs and pendulums.
