Electric Fields & Coulomb's Law

Physics II

How do charges exert force on each other, and how do we describe the field that surrounds a charge? Coulomb's Law gives the force between point charges. The electric field is the force-per-unit-charge at any point in space. These concepts are the entry point for everything else in E&M.

Electric Potential

Physics II

What is the difference between electric potential and electric potential energy? Electric potential (V) is the potential energy per unit charge at a point — a property of the field, not a specific charge. Electric potential energy (U = qV) is what a specific charge has at that location. This distinction trips up most students, and it matters for every capacitor and circuit problem.

Capacitors & Circuits

Physics II

How do you analyze a circuit with multiple resistors, capacitors, or both? For resistors in series and parallel, you combine them into an equivalent resistance and apply Ohm's Law. For multi-loop circuits, Kirchhoff's Laws give you a system of equations — one per loop, one per node.
